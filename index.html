About the data

🟢 Live data — Istanbul Kapalıçarşı (Grand Bazaar): Real-time buy and sell prices from the Istanbul physical gold market, sourced directly from the Kapalıçarşı price feed. The Istanbul gold premium over international spot price typically ranges from 0% to 5% depending on local demand, currency volatility, and import conditions.

🟠 Calculated data: Prices for Shanghai (SGE), Mumbai (MCX/IBJA), Tokyo (TOCOM), Dubai Gold Souk and Jakarta (Antam) are calculated as the international spot price × local currency exchange rate, plus known structural premiums. These represent close approximations of real local prices.

Why do physical gold prices differ from the spot price? The international spot price (LBMA/COMEX) reflects paper gold trading in London and New York. Physical gold markets in Asia and the Middle East trade real metal and reflect actual supply and demand — including import duties, taxes, local demand cycles, and currency effects. The Shanghai Gold Exchange (SGE) premium is closely watched as an indicator of Chinese physical demand. India applies approximately 13% duty+GST on gold imports, creating a structural premium. Japan (TOCOM) and Dubai typically trade close to spot.

Data sources: gold-api.com (spot prices) · frankfurter.app (FX rates) · Kapalıçarşı API (Istanbul live data)

About GoldPremium.org

GoldPremium.org is the only free tool that compares physical gold prices across major global markets in real time. Unlike paper gold (futures and ETFs), physical gold markets in Istanbul, Shanghai, Mumbai, Tokyo and Dubai reflect genuine supply and demand for real metal — and often trade at significant premiums or discounts to the international LBMA/COMEX spot price.

Tracking the gold premium across markets is valuable for investors, traders and anyone buying or selling physical gold internationally. A rising Shanghai premium often signals strong Chinese physical demand. An elevated Istanbul premium reflects local currency dynamics and import conditions. The India premium is largely structural due to government import duties.
